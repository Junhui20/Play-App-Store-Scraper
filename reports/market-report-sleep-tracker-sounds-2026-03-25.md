## 7. MONETIZATION STRATEGY

### What Competitors Get WRONG
Based on 97 pricing complaints across 5 analyzed apps:

1. **Subscription model is hated** - "I cannot add more subscriptions to my life" is a recurring sentiment
2. **Paywalling basic features** - Recording your own sleep sounds behind a paywall feels exploitative
3. **No free trial of premium** - Users can't evaluate before committing
4. **Hidden pricing** - Some apps require account creation before showing costs

### Recommended Model: Freemium with One-Time Purchase

**Free Tier (Forever Free):**
- Full sound library (15-20 sounds)
- Sound mixer (up to 3 layers)
- Sleep timer
- Offline playback
- No ads ever
- No account required

**Premium (One-Time Purchase: $4.99-$6.99):**
- Unlimited sound layers (5+)
- Sleep recorder with snoring detection
- Save unlimited presets
- Advanced timer (gradual fade, smart alarm)
- Export recordings (share with doctor)
- Binaural beats library
- Priority access to new sounds

**Why This Works:**
- Users actively praise apps that are "free, ad-free, no login" (see Calm Sounds' 93.5% positive rate)
- One-time purchase addresses the #1 complaint (subscription fatigue)
- Free tier is generous enough to build word-of-mouth
- Premium features target power users and medical use cases
- Price point ($4.99-6.99) is below competitor annual subscriptions ($20-60/year)

**Optional Revenue:**
- Donation/tip jar button (users of Calm Sounds literally asked for this)
- Seasonal sound packs ($1.99 each) - Holiday ambiance, ASMR, etc.
